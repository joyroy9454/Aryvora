Weekly Tech Digest – May 29, 2026

Anthropic hits $965B valuation, Claude Opus 4.8 drops, big tech capex hits $725B, and more this week in tech.

Overview#

This week in tech: Anthropic surpassed OpenAI as the world’s most valuable AI start-up, Claude Opus 4.8 topped benchmarks, big tech spending hit record levels, and Illinois passed the strongest US AI regulation law. Here’s what you need to know.

Top Updates This Week#

1. Anthropic Becomes World’s Most Valuable AI Start-Up at $965B#

Category: AI News

Anthropic has surpassed OpenAI to become the world’s most valuable AI start-up, reaching a $965 billion valuation. The surge comes amid strong enterprise adoption of Claude and growing demand for AI safety-focused alternatives to OpenAI’s GPT models.

Why it matters: This shifts the AI landscape significantly. Anthropic’s rise signals that enterprises are prioritizing AI safety and reliability, not just raw capability. For students, it means Claude is now a major player alongside ChatGPT.

What changed: Anthropic’s valuation jumped past OpenAI’s, driven by enterprise contracts in coding, research, and content moderation. Claude’s focus on “constitutional AI” and safety resonates with regulated industries.

What to do: If you’re a student learning AI tools, Claude is now a must-know alongside ChatGPT. Check out our AI tools comparison guide.

2. Claude Opus 4.8 Released with Top Benchmark Scores#

Category: AI News

Anthropic released Claude Opus 4.8 this week, topping benchmark charts across reasoning, coding, and math tasks. The model shows significant improvements in accuracy and reduced hallucinations compared to previous versions.

Why it matters: For students and developers, this means more reliable AI assistance for complex tasks like coding, research, and problem-solving.

What changed: Opus 4.8 outperforms GPT-4o and Gemini 2.0 on most standardized benchmarks, with particular strength in code generation and mathematical reasoning.

What to do: Try Claude Opus 4.8 for your next coding project or research task. Free tier available at claude.ai.

3. Big Tech Capex Hits $725B in 2026, Up 77% From Last Year#

Category: Big Tech

Alphabet, Amazon, Apple, Meta, and Microsoft are projected to spend $725 billion on capital expenditure in 2026, a 77% increase from 2025. The majority of this spending is on AI infrastructure, including data centers and custom chips.

Why it matters: This massive investment signals that AI is not a bubble — it’s the future of every major tech company. More AI tools, better infrastructure, and more jobs in AI-related fields.

What changed: Data center spending alone accounts for over $300B of this investment, with NVIDIA chips being the primary hardware powering AI workloads.

What to do: Learn cloud computing and AI skills — these companies will need thousands of qualified workers. Check our data science career guide.

4. Illinois Passes Landmark AI Regulation Law#

Category: Regulation

Illinois passed the strongest AI safety law in the US this week, requiring companies to conduct risk assessments for AI systems and disclose when AI is used in decision-making.

Why it matters: This sets a precedent for other states and potentially federal regulation. Companies developing or deploying AI will need to comply with new transparency and safety requirements.

What changed: The law requires bias audits, impact assessments, and clear disclosure when AI is used in hiring, lending, or healthcare decisions.

What to do: If you’re building AI projects, start learning about AI ethics and compliance now.

5. Nvidia Investing Billions in Emerging AI Technology#

Category: Hardware

Nvidia announced massive investments in next-generation AI hardware, including new inference chips that could make AI 10x more efficient.

Why it matters: Better AI hardware means faster, cheaper AI tools for everyone. This could make AI accessible on regular laptops and phones.

What changed: Nvidia’s new inference chips use 10x less power while delivering the same performance, potentially revolutionizing edge AI.

What to do: Keep an eye on these developments — they’ll directly affect the AI tools you use daily.

Why It Matters This Week#

This week marks a turning point in the AI industry. Anthropic surpassing OpenAI in valuation shows that the AI market is maturing beyond a single-enterprise race. The focus is shifting from “who has the biggest model” to “who has the most reliable, safe, and enterprise-ready AI.”

For students, this is excellent news. More competition means better tools, more job opportunities, and lower prices. The $725B in big tech spending means companies are hiring — and they need people who understand AI.

The new Illinois regulation also signals that AI legitimacy is growing. Governments are taking AI seriously, which means more funding for AI education and research.
